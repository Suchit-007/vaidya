MINISTRY OF AYUSH — STANDARD TREATMENT GUIDELINES (STG) FOR AYURVEDA
Authentic digitally-born reference data from official Ministry of AYUSH public health publications.

[CHUNK 101: STANDARD MANAGEMENT OF HYPERTENSION (RAKTA CHAPA / VYANAVATA VAISHAMYA)]
Case Definition: Essential hypertension is viewed clinically in Ayurveda through the prism of Vyana Vata vaishamya (dysfunction of the circulating Vata humor) paired with underlying obstruction of the internal vascular channels (Rasa-Rakta vaha srotas) by metabolic impurities (Ama) and Pitta dosha. Clinical Presentation: Patients frequently exhibit secondary symptoms such as Shirashula (headache), Bhrama (dizziness/vertigo), Anidra (insomnia), Klama (unexplained fatigue), and Krodha (heightened irritability). Ayurvedic Treatment Protocol: The primary goal is pacifying Vyana Vata and clearing vascular resistance. First-line single herbal entities include Sarpagandha (Rauwolfia serpentina) root powder administered in controlled doses of 1 to 2 grams twice daily with pure water, which directly downregulates hyperactive sympathetic pathways. Secondary compound medicines include Prabhakara Vati or Mukta Vati. Ahara-Vihara (Dietary and Lifestyle Guidance): Strict restriction of Lavana (salt intake), heavy fried foods, and pungent spices. Patients are prescribed daily practice of Shavasana (corpse pose), Anuloma-Viloma pranayama (alternate nostril breathing), and early morning walks to stabilize autonomic tone.

[CHUNK 102: STANDARD TREATMENT GUIDELINES FOR DIABETES MELLITUS (MADHUMEHA)]
Case Definition: Madhumeha is classified under Vataja Prameha, denoting an advanced metabolic disorder where vital essential tissues (Dhatus) are continuously depleted, yielding sweet-tasting urine and chronic systemic fatigue. Root factors include excessive intake of Guru (heavy), Snigdha (unctuous), and Madhura (sweet) items combined with absolute physical inactivity (Avyayama), leading to profound Kapha and Medas (adipose tissue) corruption. Clinical Presentation: Core cardinal symptoms include Prabhuta Avila Mutrata (excessive and turbid urination), Trishna (polydipsia), Kshudha (polyphagia), Daurbalya (generalized weakness), and Karapada Daha (burning sensation in the palms and soles). Treatment Protocol: First-line botanical therapy heavily mandates the use of Meshashringi (Gymnema sylvestre), known classically as the 'sugar destroyer', combined with Jambubeeja (Syzygium cumini seeds), Amalaki (Emblica officinalis), and Haridra (Curcuma longa). The combination of Amalaki and Haridra, known as Nisha-Amalaki, serves as the ultimate specific pacifier for Prameha pathways by stabilizing cellular glucose uptake and protecting micro-vascular beds. Ahara Guidance: Use of aged barley (Yava), bitter gourds (Karela), and astringent green vegetables.

[CHUNK 103: OSTEOARTHRITIS CLINICAL PATHWAYS (SANDHIGATA VATA)]
Case Definition: Sandhigata Vata is a classic degenerative musculoskeletal condition where generalized Vata dosha localizes in the major weight-bearing joints, leading to progressive destruction of the Shleshaka Kapha (articular cartilage and synovial fluid). Clinical Presentation: Cardinal manifestations documented in the guidelines include Sandhisula (joint pain specifically provoked during movement), Sandhisopha (localized mild swelling), Atopa (crepitus or cracking sounds on flexion/extension), and restricted range of motion. Treatment Protocol: Therapy necessitates systematic internal lubrication and localized structural nourishment. Formulations include Yogaraja Guggulu (2 tablets twice daily with warm water) to clear deep-seated inflammation, combined with internal administration of Maharasnadi Kwatha (herbal decoction). Externally, regular local application of Mahanarayana Taila followed by gentle Patrapinda Sveda (bolus fomentation using fresh medicinal leaves) is highly advised to restore localized joint elasticity and mitigate peripheral nociceptive signaling.
